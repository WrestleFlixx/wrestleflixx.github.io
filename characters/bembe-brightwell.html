The Origin Story — Lightning, Lies, and Rubber

Three lightning strikes before age 18. First: to the butt during a Little League All-Star game, age nine. Three weeks on a donut cushion. Second: to the arm under a picnic pavilion at the Big Sioux River, while rafting. Third: night of senior prom, hair on fire in the parking lot. This last one gave him a slight stutter.

The resulting astraphobia and general psychological damage led to weekly therapy with one Judith Framm, a crooked therapist who hypnotized Bembe into believing that an alien scientist named Dr. Zarflax had replaced his muscles with rubber, making him impervious to lightning. It was the big fat lie he needed. Bembe became a brand new man overnight — almost too confident. He accepted an invitation to Fight 2 Win, where he could very well die. Dr. Zarflax (real name: Eric McNugent, heir to McNuge Restaurant Supply) continues inventing gadgets for Bembe. They always backfire.

His ex-girlfriends — many of them employees of Yankee Candle or Bath & Body Works he dated specifically for the discount — have formed a Facebook group. They surface periodically to make his life difficult. This is ongoing.
