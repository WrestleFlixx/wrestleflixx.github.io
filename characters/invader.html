Origin — The Banishment of Nexophel

Before the body, before the wrestling ring, before the prune juice — there was Nexophel. A cosmic entity of terrible power that swept through galaxies turning armadas against themselves, sowing dread into the minds of entire civilizations, and surviving weapons forged specifically to destroy it. The Ornara Cluster tried. They failed. Many did.

The Vitaeum Council succeeded — barely. Wielding Aether's Cipher, a weapon forged from the collective will of a thousand worlds, they enacted the Banishment: Nexophel was torn from the cosmos and cast into the body of a human wrestler named Jessica Smith. A trap. A tomb. A nothing planet.

Except the tomb is conscious. And it has a WrestleFlixx TV deal.

inVader inhabits Jessie's body with growing unease at its limitations and growing fascination with the strange rules of this dimension — including the inexplicable healing speed of prune juice, the loyalty of a roommate named Riley, and the ritual combat humans call professional wrestling, which inVader has decided is the most efficient path to dominance available on this planet. It's not wrong.
