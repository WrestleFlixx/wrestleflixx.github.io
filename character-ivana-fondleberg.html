Entrance

The arena lights dim as a regal violin crescendo plays. A holographic Fondleberg Crest animation flickers on the tron. Ivana emerges atop a raised platform, draped in a white fur-trimmed robe and diamond-encrusted sunglasses. She removes the robe to reveal a shimmering silver-and-gold leotard, then tosses it at a referee like they're her personal valet. She skips down the ramp, blowing kisses to booing fans, occasionally snatching a drink from the crowd to pretend to sip before spitting it out in disgust. At ringside, she performs a flawless cartwheel into the ring.

Gimmick

Ivana is a spoiled, genetically gifted prodigy who treats wrestling like her latest vanity project. She's obsessed with proving she's "self-made" despite her family's influence, but won't hesitate to bribe officials or distract referees to maintain her image. Her athleticism is undeniable — she'll perform a standing backflip to avoid a clothesline, then lecture the opponent on "proper form."

Biography

Born into obscene wealth, Ivana was groomed for greatness from childhood. By 16, she'd medaled in junior Olympics for gymnastics and fencing. By 20, she'd grown bored of "pedestrian sports" and set her sights on wrestling — much to her parents' horror. After a year of private training with retired legends (paid for by daddy, though she'll deny it), she debuted in Europe's LUXE Wrestling as "The Heiress of Hurt." Despite her inexperience, her natural agility and ruthless opportunism earned her a cult following. Now she's storming the global scene, demanding title shots and leaving trails of defeated veterans in her wake.
